1.Ease to develop for developers [40 words]
	Tableau: Best. You can create the graph just by clicking and dragging.
	Excel: Medium. You can adjust the data structure can use the templates. 
	D3: Worst. Need strong background knowledge. Can be time-consuming.

2.Ease to maintain the visualization for developers (e.g., difficulty of the maintenance of the product 
as the requirements change, the data changes, the hosting platform changes, etc.) [40 words]
	Tableau: Good. New versions support old ones.
	Excel: Best. Everyone has excel. New versions support old ones.
	D3: Worst. The syntax changes in new versions.

3.Usability of visualization developed for end users [40 words]
	Tableau: Medium. You can enjoy the picture.
	Excel: Worst. The pictures are usually simple, sometimes ugly.
	D3: Best. Support good interactivity with users.

4.Scalability of visualization to “large” datasets [40 words]
	Tableau: Best. Handles large dataset well.
	Excel: Worst. Can be slow and take a lot of space.
	D3: Medium. Consume a lot of RAM.

5.System requirements to run the visualization (e.g., browsers, OS, software licensing) for end users [40 words]
	Tableau: Medium. License is needed.
	Excel: Best. Everyone has excel.
	D3: Worst. You need a local server or do some mysterious adjustment to your brower.
